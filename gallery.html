Hyoseris frutescens

Hyoseris frutescens, commonly known as the Canary Island lettuce, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands and Madeira. This unique plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh conditions. Hyoseris frutescens plays a crucial role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and sustenance for various wildlife. Additionally, its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes, contributing to the biodiversity of these island environments.

Maltese Sea Lavender

Limonium melitense, commonly known as Maltese Sea Lavender, is a plant species endemic to the Maltese Islands in the Mediterranean. This perennial herb is recognized for its distinctive clusters of small, lilac-colored flowers and succulent leaves. Thriving in coastal habitats, Limonium melitense plays a vital role in stabilizing sand dunes and contributing to local biodiversity. Conservation efforts are underway to protect this unique species, as it faces threats from habitat loss and human activities.
